History of Our Madrasa

Our madrasa was established in 1954 by Muḥammadullāh ibn Idrīs ibn Akram ad-Dīn al-Miyānjī, commonly known as Hafezzī Huzūr. A prominent Islamic leader and politician, Hafezzī Huzūr laid the foundation for a nurturing environment where students could grow academically and spiritually.

In 1941 Mosque was established, in 1954 Madrasa was established, in 1981 Arrival of Hafizji Huzur Rah

Early Life and Education

Born in 1895 in Ludhua, Bangladesh, Muhammadullah's education began at Fatehpur Primary School and continued at various madrasas, including Mazahir Uloom in Saharanpur. He completed his studies at Darul Uloom Deoband in 1923, earning a high-class certificate.

Political Career

Hafezzī Huzūr founded the Nizam-e-Islam Party in 1952, advocating for the incorporation of Islamic principles in governance. He also established the Bangladesh Khilafat Andolan in 1981, striving for a political landscape rooted in Islamic values.

Legacy

Hafezzī Huzūr's contributions to both education and politics have left a lasting impact. His vision continues to inspire us as we uphold the values he instilled in our madrasa.

Land Donation

The land for our madrasa was generously donated by Late Amir Ali Bhuiyan, whose legacy remains a vital part of our history.
